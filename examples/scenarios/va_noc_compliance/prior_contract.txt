VA OFFICE OF INFORMATION AND TECHNOLOGY (OIT) — ORGANIZATIONAL OVERVIEW

Compiled from public sources, industry day materials, and partner intelligence.
Last updated: March 2026

LEADERSHIP
- Assistant Secretary for IT / CIO: Vacant as of March 2026; Acting CIO duties delegated to Principal Deputy Assistant Secretary Karen E. Fields.
- Deputy CIO for IT Operations: Mark L. Sweeney. Responsible for day-to-day IT service delivery, enterprise operations metrics, and operational risk posture.
- Deputy CIO for Information Security (CISO): Dr. Rochelle N. Grant. Oversees VA CSOC, Zero Trust Architecture implementation, cyber incident management, and FISMA reporting.
- Deputy CIO for Enterprise Program Management: Thomas J. Avery. Oversees major IT acquisitions, governance boards, and contract portfolio planning.

NETWORK AND INFRASTRUCTURE DIVISION
Reports to Deputy CIO for IT Operations. Responsible for:
- Enterprise network architecture and engineering
- WAN/LAN infrastructure management
- Data center operations
- Cloud infrastructure (VAEC)
- Current NOC contract: held by Frontier Nexus Federal Services, expires Q4 FY2026

Division structure and influence map:
- Executive Director, Network and Infrastructure: Sets technical strategy and approves annual modernization priorities.
- Director, Enterprise Networking: Owns campus core standards, WAN strategy, and VISN-level integration policy.
- Director, Operations Engineering: Owns network reliability targets, major incident review board, and change governance quality gates.
- Director, Cloud Connectivity and Edge Services: Coordinates cloud ingress/egress, east-west segmentation requirements, and policy alignment with CSOC.

KEY PROCUREMENT CONTACTS
- Contracting Officer: VA Technology Acquisition Center (TAC), Eatontown, NJ.
- COR: TBD (typically assigned from Network and Infrastructure Division).
- Technical Evaluation: Conducted by Network Engineering team leads and operations managers.
- Source Selection Advisor: Usually aligned to Enterprise Program Management for service contracts above $50M total potential value.

BUDGET CONTEXT
VA OIT FY2026 budget: approximately $6.2 billion.
Infrastructure and Operations: estimated 35% of IT budget.
NOC services historically funded under IT Operations allocation, with supplemental modernization funding for tooling transitions and cloud connectivity enhancements.

Observed spending signals relevant to this procurement:
- Strong emphasis on reducing avoidable outages at medical centers and high-volume outpatient facilities.
- Increased scrutiny of labor-only proposals that do not include automation-enabled productivity gains.
- Preference for offerors demonstrating concrete staffing retention controls and measurable reduction in mean-time-to-restore.

INCUMBENT INFORMATION
Current NOC services provided under contract 36C10X21D0041 / Task Order 36C10X21F0158, awarded FY2021.
Estimated team size: 20-25 FTEs.
Reported challenges with staffing retention and P1 response time compliance (per FY2025 quarterly reviews).
Additional observed incumbent issues:
- Higher-than-target attrition on overnight shift analysts.
- Delayed closure quality on recurring WAN carrier incidents.
- Uneven documentation quality during surge changes tied to SD-WAN cutovers.

STRATEGIC DIRECTION
- VA migrating enterprise monitoring from SolarWinds to ServiceNow ITOM (FY2027).
- SD-WAN deployment underway (replacing legacy MPLS at approximately 400 sites through FY2027).
- Zero Trust Architecture initiative requires tighter micro-segmentation and policy-based traffic controls.
- VA CSOC expanding threat hunting capabilities; closer NOC/SOC integration expected.
- Enterprise push for standardized post-incident problem management tied to repeat-event prevention metrics.

TEAMING / COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
Expected full and open competition with strong participation from existing VA primes.
Known interested parties:
- Frontier Nexus Federal Services (incumbent) — likely to re-compete.
- Peraton — strong VA enterprise operations footprint.
- SAIC — recent VA network engineering and integration wins.
- Leidos — VISN-level support contracts and staffing bench depth.
- Booz Allen Hamilton — likely as a teammate/subcontractor in cyber-integration-heavy proposals.

OUR COMPETITIVE POSITION (APEX MERIDIAN TECHNOLOGIES)
Strengths:
- OASIS+ vehicle access and recent civilian agency operations wins.
- Relevant past performance (DOI Help Desk, DHS Cybersecurity Operations, SSA ITSM modernization).
- Key personnel with direct VA experience (David Park served as VA network architect from 2019-2021).
- Demonstrated SOC/NOC operating model that can support integrated incident handling.

Gaps:
- No direct prime VA enterprise NOC past performance.
- Need to demonstrate mature SolarWinds Orion and ServiceNow ITOM coexistence capability.
- Staffing plan must show credible 24/7 coverage depth and low attrition risk controls.

RECOMMENDED BID THEMES
1. Clinical mission continuity first: frame all NOC outcomes around patient-care service availability and restoration speed.
2. Transition certainty: provide a phased transition approach with objective readiness gates and measurable risk burn-down.
3. Tooling modernization readiness: show practical SolarWinds-to-ServiceNow ITOM migration support without service disruption.
4. Workforce stability: present retention incentives, structured shift differential strategy, and bench-backed surge coverage.
5. Integrated cyber operations: emphasize repeatable NOC/CSOC handoff patterns and joint major incident playbooks.
