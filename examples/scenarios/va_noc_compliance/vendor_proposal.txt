VA NOC Managed Services Proposal Response (Draft)

1. Program Overview
Our team will deliver a 24x7x365 VA-aligned NOC operation with centralized monitoring, incident management, change coordination, and executive reporting for the nationwide VA enterprise network.

2. Network Monitoring and Event Management
- We will monitor WAN circuits, core/distribution switching, firewalls, load balancers, DNS/DHCP/NTP, wireless, and VPN services through VA-provided monitoring tools.
- We will tune thresholds to reduce false positives and protect patient-care-impacting alert sensitivity.
- We will review correlation rules monthly and after major incidents.
- We will remediate identified monitoring gaps on a documented action plan.

3. Incident Management
- Incidents will be triaged and tracked under ITIL-aligned workflows with severity, impact, affected site(s), action timeline, and closure notes.
- P1 and P2 incidents will include active bridge facilitation and routine status updates to stakeholders.
- We will provide after-action documentation for high-severity events.

4. Change Management Support
- We will prepare implementation plans and execute approved maintenance-window changes.
- Post-change verification and rollback support will be included for normal and emergency changes.
- CMDB updates will be performed by the operations team.

5. Reporting and Capacity Planning
- Daily operations summaries and weekly dashboards will be delivered.
- Monthly executive reporting will include incident trends, SLA posture, and improvement opportunities.
- Capacity forecasting will include VPN growth and WAN utilization planning.

6. Staffing Model
- Day shift staffing: 6 analysts.
- Night shift staffing: 4 analysts.
- Weekend/holiday coverage: 4 analysts per shift.
- NOC Manager and Senior Network Analyst key personnel will be assigned at award.
- The team will maintain workforce continuity planning and surge support rotation.

7. Service Levels
- NOC operational availability target: 99.99% monthly (excluding approved maintenance).
- Monitoring onboarding target for newly registered devices: within 24 hours.
- Documentation and CMDB quality checks will be included in quarterly governance.

8. Security and Compliance
- Personnel will follow VA Directive/Handbook 6500 controls and complete annual role-based cybersecurity training.
- Team members will use VA-approved remote access methods and support FISMA/ATO documentation needs.
- Potential security incidents will be escalated through VA channels.

9. Transition Approach
- We will execute transition-in with knowledge transfer, parallel operations, and independent operations phases.
- Transition-out support and editable handoff documentation will be provided at contract completion.

10. Assumptions and Open Items
- Public Trust onboarding timelines depend on VA adjudication lead time.
- Final response/resolution SLA minute thresholds and report due times will be confirmed at kickoff.
